# Article Value Inspector (AVIM)

## Philosophy

In an age of information overload, most online content is noise. The skill provides a systematic, five-dimensional "funnel" to filter articles efficiently, separating high-value signal from low-value noise. It integrates four powerful intellectual traditions into one practical inspection workflow.

**Core principle**: Do not passively absorb (sponge-style). Actively interrogate every article before investing reading time (gold-panning style).

## The Five-Dimensional Filter (5D-Funnel)

Each article is inspected across five dimensions, in order of priority:

| Dimension | Source Books | Core Question | Time Budget |
|-----------|--------------|-------------|-------------|
| **D1 — Depth (本质)** | First Principles + Mao "Essence through Phenomena" | Does the article touch the root cause, or stay at surface? | 30 sec |
| **D2 — Data (事实)** | Mao "Seek Truth from Facts" + Critical Thinking "Evidence" | Are claims grounded in reliable facts and investigation? | 1 min |
| **D3 — Logic (逻辑)** | Critical Thinking + Asking Right Questions | Is the argument structurally sound? Any fallacies? | 1 min |
| **D4 — Assumption (假设)** | First Principles + Asking Right Questions | Are hidden assumptions identified and challenged? | 30 sec |
| **D5 — Utility (实用)** | Mao "Practice Theory" + Asking Right Questions | Is it actionable? Is critical information omitted? | 30 sec |

**Total inspection time**: ~3.5 minutes for a go/no-go decision.

## Quick Start Workflow

### Step 1: Intentional Pause (5 seconds)
Before reading, ask: **"What do I need from this article?"** If the article does not match your current learning or decision needs, skip it regardless of quality.

### Step 2: Five-Dimensional Scan (3.5 minutes)
Run each dimension in sequence. The first failing dimension is usually sufficient for a reject decision.

**D1 — Depth Scan**: Does the article analyze root causes, or merely describe symptoms? Does it use first-principles deconstruction, or rely on analogy and conventional wisdom? Does it practice Mao's "去粗取精, 去伪存真, 由此及彼, 由表及里" (discard the dross, select the essential, proceed from one thing to another, penetrate from surface to essence)?

**D2 — Data Scan**: Does the article cite primary sources or original investigation? Is the data traceable? Is it cherry-picked? Are statistics manipulated? See [references/five_dimensions_checklist.md](references/five_dimensions_checklist.md) for detailed D2 checklist.

**D3 — Logic Scan**: Can you state the author's conclusion in one sentence? Are there clear reasons supporting it? Is there a logical leap? Scan for common fallacies (ad hominem, straw man, false dilemma, post hoc, etc.). See [references/fallacy_quick_ref.md](references/fallacy_quick_ref.md).

**D4 — Assumption Scan**: What must the author believe for their conclusion to follow? Are those beliefs stated and defended, or hidden and unquestioned? Does the article challenge industry consensus, or parrot it?

**D5 — Utility Scan**: Does the article provide actionable insight? What important counter-evidence or limitations are omitted? Is the conclusion overgeneralized?

### Step 3: Rate and Route (30 seconds)

| Grade | Criteria | Action |
|-------|----------|--------|
| **A** | Passes all 5 dimensions; high evidence quality; challenges assumptions; admits limitations | Read carefully + take notes |
| **B** | Passes 4 dimensions; minor issues in one area | Read selectively; extract only the solid parts |
| **C** | Passes 2–3 dimensions; significant weaknesses | Skim for awareness only; do not trust conclusions |
| **D** | Fails D1 or D3 (depth or logic); or passes only 0–1 dimensions | Reject immediately; do not invest time |

**Critical override rule**: If D1 (Depth) and D3 (Logic) both fail, automatic D regardless of other dimensions.

## Fundamental Mindsets from Source Books

1. **Gold-panning over sponge-absorbing** (from *Asking the Right Questions*): Do not absorb everything. Interrogate everything.
2. **Strong-sense critical thinking** (from *Asking the Right Questions*): Evaluate all assertions, especially those you already agree with.
3. **Seek truth from facts** (from *Mao*): Start from objective reality, not from subjective imagination, emotion, or dogma.
4. **No investigation, no right to speak** (from *Mao*): Claims without investigation foundation are empty.
5. **Practice is the sole criterion for testing truth** (from *Mao*): Falsifiable, testable claims are superior to untestable speculation.
6. **First principles over analogy** (from *First Principles*): Break problems to fundamental truths and rebuild; reject reasoning by analogy for deep analysis.
7. **Argument = Reasons + Conclusion** (from *Critical Thinking*): A claim without reasons is an assertion, not an argument.
8. **Every argument rests on assumptions** (from *Critical Thinking*): If assumptions collapse, the argument collapses.
